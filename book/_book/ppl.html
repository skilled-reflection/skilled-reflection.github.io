Skilled Reflection

Chapter 4 PPL

  1. Author’s note: The concepts in this chapter are under active development.

  2. Numbers 48-53 would be the basis for a revised chapter.

  3. Think of PPL (PPL) as seeds that clarify and cultivate your garden or affect your garden in other ways.

  4. Maybe they are your friend, and need help with some unruly vines, or maybe you just like their apples. Sometimes, PPL are like water, bringing extra life to needy crops.

  5. Sometimes PPL are careless and track weeds in on their boots, or over- encourage your interests until you sprain an ankle, or come to your garden and distract you, make you anxious or bored or excited. How are PPL affecting your garden?

  6. Everything that happens in their garden could be thought of as a seed in yours.

  7. How much TIME your visit takes and what you get are like TIME spent on a plot of soil of that person, and the fruit that comes of your TIME.

  8. Where do you get seeds for FOOD from and how did you know to plant them?

  9. From other PPL, right? No silly, you don’t know how to garden a sandwich!

  10. Lucky for you, many of the most important crops, products, and savings are already grown, prepared and handed to you–in exchange for money, which you have from spending TIME on a much larger, collective garden called society, doing some very specialized thing called WORK.

4.1 WORK

  1. You’ll WORK about 80,000 hours in your life in exchange for already grown apples and baked CAKE, and a toilet to take your poop somewhere else.

  2. WORK pays the broadest insurance: money.

  3. Money is traded for maintenance of SELF and CAKE.

  4. It takes 80,000 hours of your TIME.

  5. WORK is made to serve NORMS, not you.

  6. Relatedly, NORMS assume you will WORK for money for goods.

  7. NORMS are all actions assumed of (or about) the “average” person.

  8. Language, agreed usage of WORDS, is made from NORMS.

  9. NORMS may or may not always be the most reliable way for you.

  10. NORMS are the web of FORCES of all actions of all PPL, including WORK, religion, popular attitudes, and DOUBTS.

  11. When you are individual, NORMS push against you.

  12. A FORCE is any cause of change. 20.1. A FORCE underlies every action involved in a GOAL, yours or otherwise. 20.2. There are forces within your control, and forces outside.

  13. MAINTENANCE is the COST of FORCE to neither move toward nor away from a GOAL.

  14. ALIGNMENT is when NORM or natural FORCE causes your GOAL to be more likely. 22.1. To ALIGN is to adjust your GOAL to be more like another force, usually one acting against your GOAL. 22.2. Study how forces WORK against you.

  15. Relationships (RLTP) are GOALS about PPL (PPL).

  16. Good ones are aligned with your GOALS.

  17. Bad ones COST more.

  18. A RLTP is a reciprocal pair of BETS, yours of them, and vice versa.

  19. RLTPS, especially family members, coordinate many GOALS for SAVINGS.

  20. Pick WORK and RLTP that maximize your other PRIS including possibly one that maximizes TIME and money to apply to other PRIS.

  21. Pick friends the same way.

  22. Consider your actions and choices about PPL carefully.

  23. Maximally ALIGN with NORMS with least compromise to PRIS.

  24. ALIGN (get along) with PPL.

4.2 The Classroom

Note: If this section is confusing, revisit it after “Ed.”

  1. (No one chose to be born.)

  2. Everyone begins life with their own unnamed and unanswered problems.

  3. Books can only tell a student what the answer is not (quite).

  4. The gap between problem and book answers is left for the student to “apply.”

  5. A better approach would be to let the student define the problem through their reality and life GOALS.

  6. Let them write the PLAN and teach them only what is needed to succeed.

  7. Imagine a perfect course exists, designed to teach you to fulfill your specific ambitions.

  8. Every aspect of what you NEED to know, that is known and communicable, is the only thing written.

  9. Everything that cannot be known but must be discovered or practiced, is laid out as a set of instructions, described in the WORDS that maximize the learning opportunity, and your progress.

  10. Rather than a course in a classroom, the perfect class is a manual to reference as you live your life, or at least until you’ve internalized its contents:

  11. when to take a break to strategize your decisions, lessons on what opportunities to watch for and resist, and so on.

  12. Any social role you wish to take on, artist, engineer, therapist, insurance salesperson, reliable partner, is customized intimately, curated perfectly for what you need.

  13. Any relationship or interpersonal skill that is realistically possible for you is preceded with the guidance and education that prepares you emotionally to choose the right experiences that set you up to be most likely to find and make the most of opportunities to share yourself with another.

  14. People, are the part of a theory of life that require the most care, and least words.

  15. Unlike the nature of our actions which play unconsciously and imply bets,

  16. Common, bad people activity is the habit of over-emphasizing mutual bets, and

  17. which fall out of alignment with actions.

  18. Instead, consider a relationship to be only the actions that are reciprocated;

  19. and call this the relationship ‘contract.’
