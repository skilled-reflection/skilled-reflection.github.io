Chapter 4 PPL

4.1 Who are PPL

  1. Just like you, others are trying to figure out what goes in their garden.*

* You could think of people as seeds in your garden, but this is an over-application of seeds; and its unnecessary. 2. Maybe they are your friend, and need help with some unruly vines, or maybe you just like their apples. 3. Sometimes, PPL will contribute to your goals, as if bringing water to crops you didn’t recognize need them. 4. Sometimes PPL will try to water your crops when they don’t need watering. 5. Sometimes visiting their garden will bring up your morale, or get you a bag of apples to take home. 6. Sometimes you’ll need to water some crops that need to be watered, because the sight of their neglect cannot be ignored. 7. Keep in mind that the challenges that sometimes overwhelm you are the same kind others deal with, but not always the same amount and at the same time. 8. How are PPL affecting your garden?

4.2 WORK

  1. Where do you get seeds for FOOD from and how did you know to plant them?

  2. From other PPL, right? No silly, you don’t know how to garden a sandwich!

  3. Lucky for you, many of the most important crops you want are already grown, prepared and handed to you–in exchange for money.

  4. Money is traded for maintenance of, or insurance for SELF and CAKE, like apples, miracle medical procedures, and a toilet to take your poop somewhere else.

  5. WORK is performing a specific task on a collective garden, like an institution’s in exchange for money.

  6. You’ll WORK about 80,000 hours in your life.

  7. Whether or not you like it, or it directly fulfills garden needs, WORK is made to serve NORMS, not you.

  8. NORMS are all actions assumed of (or about) the “average” person. They are the web of FORCES of all actions of all PPL, including WORK, religion, popular attitudes, and DOUBTS.

  9. Relatedly, NORMS assume you will WORK for money for goods.

  10. Language, agreed usage of WORDS, is made from NORMS.

  11. NORMS push against individuality (except where it provides a lucrative job opportunity).

  12. A FORCE is any cause of change.

  13. A FORCE underlies every action involved in a GOAL, yours or otherwise.

  14. There are forces within your control, and forces outside.

  15. MAINTENANCE is the COST of FORCE to neither move toward nor away from a GOAL.

  16. ALIGNMENT is when NORM or natural FORCE causes your GOAL to be more likely.

    1. To ALIGN is to adjust your GOAL to be more like another force, usually one acting against your GOAL.
    2. Study how forces WORK against you.

  17. Relationships (RLTP) are GOALS about PPL (PPL).

  18. Good ones are aligned with your GOALS.

  19. Bad ones COST more.

  20. A RLTP is a reciprocal pair of BETS, yours of them, and vice versa.

  21. RLTPS, especially family members, coordinate many GOALS for SAVINGS.

  22. Pick WORK and RLTP that maximize your other PRIS including possibly one that maximizes TIME and money to apply to other PRIS.

  23. Pick friends the same way.

  24. Consider your actions and choices about PPL carefully.

  25. Maximally ALIGN with NORMS with least compromise to PRIS.

  26. ALIGN (get along) with PPL.

4.3 The Classroom

Note: If this section is confusing, revisit it after “Ed.”

  1. (No one chose to be born.)

  2. Everyone begins life with their own unnamed and unanswered problems.

  3. Books can only tell a student what the answer is not (quite).

  4. The gap between problem and book answers is left for the student to “apply.”

  5. A better approach would be to let the student define the problem through their reality and life GOALS.

  6. Let them write the PLAN and teach them only what is needed to succeed.

  7. Imagine a perfect course exists, designed to teach you to fulfill your specific ambitions.

  8. Every aspect of what you NEED to know, that is known and communicable, is the only thing written.

  9. Everything that cannot be known but must be discovered or practiced, is laid out as a set of instructions, described in the WORDS that maximize the learning opportunity, and your progress.

  10. Rather than a course in a classroom, the perfect class is a manual to reference as you live your life, or at least until you’ve internalized its contents:

  11. when to take a break to strategize your decisions, lessons on what opportunities to watch for and resist, and so on.

  12. Any social role you wish to take on, artist, engineer, therapist, insurance salesperson, reliable partner, is customized intimately, curated perfectly for what you need.

  13. Any relationship or interpersonal skill that is realistically possible for you is preceded with the guidance and education that prepares you emotionally to choose the right experiences that set you up to be most likely to find and make the most of opportunities to share yourself with another.

  14. People, are the part of a theory of life that require the most care, and least words.

  15. Unlike the nature of our actions which play unconsciously and imply bets,

  16. Common, bad people activity is the habit of over-emphasizing mutual bets, and

  17. which fall out of alignment with actions.

  18. Instead, consider a relationship to be only the actions that are reciprocated;

  19. and call this the relationship ‘contract.’
